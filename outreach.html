Outreach

The GBIC committee offers many opportunites for graduate students to inspire the next generation of scientists in Bioinformatics.

Please see our Calendar for any upcoming events.

Annual events

Expanding Your Horizons

We partner with Expanding Your Horizons to host social, hands-on workshops for young women scientists to learn programming and genomics.

STEM Expo Day @ Petco Park

In partnership with UBIC and UCSD Scientista, we hold booths at the annual STEM Expo Day @ Petco Park where we showcase our research and engage kids with exciting science and engineering activities.

More opportunities

At your convenience

  • Volunteer at Salk Institute
    • Mobile Science Laboratory: Travel all over San Diego to bring biotechnology to middle school students in a 3-day curriculum recurring every other week
    • SciChats@Salk: Have lunch video-chats with a group of students
    • Community events
  • Fleet Science Center
    • Two Scientists Walk Into a Bar: socialize with local scientists at a local bar.

Regular/weekly commitments

  • Reality Changers
    • Tutoring or college prep mentor of youth from disadvantageous backgrounds
  • Homework help @ public libraries
    • Not currently onboarding due to COVID but still accepting applications
    • There are different listings but most commitments fall somewhere in 1-6 hours a week for minimum of 6-9months for K-12 tutoring all subjects
    • For listed postings: click here (search "homework")
  • Barrio Logan College Institute
    • After-school tutoring on any area of study
  • Preuss School
    • Tutor - 1-6 hours per week
    • Mentor - 30-45min per week - 1 year commitment
  • Jacobs Undergraduate Mentoring Program (JUMP)
    • Mentor a group of UCSD 1st and 2nd year undergraduates
  • Rosa Parks Tutoring Program
    • Usually begin participation in the fall, run by UCSD graduate students, but you can reach out and ask if they would like any help
    • For more info: email rptpatucsd@gmail.com
